Angles at the Mirador

These are my estimations of some azimuths of directions visible in photos at the Mirador. The idea is to measure the azimuth of the summit visible in photos at the Mirador and determine the horizontal angle of view of Canon PowerShot SX270 HS.

Photo M-1

point A - azimuth 81.69 (the center of photo 500)
point E - azimuth 90 (East)
point B - azimuth 106.79 (summit)

Photo M-2

point A - azimuth 97.07 deg. (the center of photo 501)
point E - azimuth 90 deg. (East)
point B - azimuth 106.79 deg. (summit)
point C - azimuth 124.59 deg. (the center of photo 10)
point D - azimuth 127.69 deg. (the left side of photo 499)

The azimuth of the summit = 90 + 16.79 deg. = 106.79 deg.

The horizontal size of the sensor of the camera h = 6.17 mm
focal length f = 4.5 mm
Horizontal angle of view = 2 * arctan h/(2*f) = 2 arctan 6.17/(2 * 4.5) = 68.85 deg.
