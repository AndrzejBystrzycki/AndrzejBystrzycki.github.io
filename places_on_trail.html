Places on the El Pianista trail and beyond the Mirador

Here I gathered information about places where Kris and Lisanne took their photos. The next step is to compare times of girls's hike with times of another hike. EXIF times of photos from the wikiloc website are used for this purpose.

I use the following sources of coordinates:

A. Coordinates from EXIF data of a photo that I found on the Internet.
B. Coordinates from a wikiloc website. Such coordinates are obtained as follows: you find an individual photo on a wikiloc website. The photo resembles the place you look for. You position the mouse cursor at the arrow at the photo on the list on the wikiloc website you found. See here, for example. That is the arrow inside the rectangle containing the sign "End (and start) of the trail at restaurant El Pianista". There is the sign "Elevation. Coordinates lat, lon (WGS84): 8.8083540 -82.427796" in the hint (or tip) which appears after you position the mouse cursor at the arrow. The hint contains the coordinates you look for.

I do not see any suspicious speed of Kris and Lisanne's hike on individual segments of the trail. The speed of the girls's hike varies from 103% to 145% of the corresponding speed during the compared hike. That is not especially unusual.

I do not claim that EXIF times of photos taken by the girls were not manipulated. It is suspicious that Kris and Lisanne reached the Mirador very quickly. It was mentioned in the article. In addition, there are times between individual photos that seem to be suspicious. An example of that is the analysis carried out by Juan Perea concerning photos 499 and 500 (see here). A similar situation occurred in photos 501 and 502. In this case, the time between EXIF times of the photos is 5 seconds, which is shorter than the time between photos 499 and 500. Kris had to go to another side of the Mirador during those 5 seconds. Another example is the analysis of the time between photos 500 and 501 described in the article. It would be good to carry out a similar experiment on the whole sequence of photos 499, 500, 501 and 502. Another suspicious sequence of times is the sequence of photos 480-486. That is 7 photos in 40 seconds. That is the fastest sequence of photos during the hike.

Further analysis should be connected with EXIF data, the camera settings and EXIF times. An example of that is photo 504. The photo combination illustrates that photo 504 should have been taken with a different focal length, because photo 504 has a different angle of view. The red rectangle in photo 502 contains the view in photo 504. The difference betweeen EXIF times of photo 503 and 504 is 5 seconds. If photo 503 and 504 have different angles of views (as I write this, photo 503 is not known in public), then the focal length had to be changed between the photos, and that requires additional time for the change of the camera settings.

Now it is worth to estimate the time at which the girls started to go beyond the Mirador. Let's start with the time from the Mirador to the place where photo 505 was taken. Here I estimated the time at about 2 minutes. That is the time that I estimated for photo 506 (492 according to the old numbering). We know already that photos 505 and 506 must have been taken at the places that are close to each other. That would mean that the girls started to descend after the Mirador at about 13:18:30 (about 2 minutes before EXIF time of photo 505, which is 13:20:33). That means that they reached the first stream crossing after 36 minutes and 20 seconds. It took Kris' parents 45 minutes to get from the Mirador to the stream crossing (see the film 11:25). In this case, the quotient of speeds is 124%, which is the same (!) as for the above comparison (see the table above, the segment "start of trail - Mirador").
